Instrumental Renaissance Dances

Pieces from the Renaissance era appeal to me because they were written before the major/minor system was really solidified in Western music. Most every phrase ends in a major triad (or an open fifth, which is neither major nor minor), but often the phrases take turns that are unexpected to our modern ears. To my thinking, this music is so old that it feels, in a sense, refreshingly new.

Praetorius

Michael Praetorius (1571ish - 1621) was a giant figure in Renaissance music. Like Bach, he was a German Christian organist composer.
