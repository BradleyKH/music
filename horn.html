Selected Works Featuring the Horn

Classical Works

Brahms

One of my favorite composers, Brahms loved to use the horn lavishly in his symphonies.

  • Symphony No. 1, mvt. 2 (6:23-7:28!)
  • Symphony No. 1, mvt. 4 (2:45!)
  • Symphony No. 3, mvt. 3 (3:33!)
Handel, Water Music

Handel's Water Music is a delightful set of three suites. King George I couldn't get enough of it!

  • Suite in F major, Variation 1
  • Suite in F major, Variation 2
Holst, Jupiter from The Planets

Horns have some beautiful melodies in this variegated movement. They even imitate laughter!

Mendelssohn, Nocturne from A Midsummer Night's Dream

Mendelssohn wrote incidental music for this Shakespearean play. I believe this movement has the longest horn solo in the standard orchestral repertoire.

Respighi, Pines of Rome (excerpts)

This colorful piece has some challenging horn parts. The epic crescendo at the end is an inspiration to all brass players - and really to all human beings with a pulse. This is an abbreviated version featured in Fantasia 2000.

Rossini, Semiramide Overture

After a brief introduction, this overture features an extended horn quartet!

Tchaikovsky, Symphony No. 5, mvt. 2

I'm not a huge fan of Tchaikovsky, but this movement features an exquisite horn solo very early on.

The London Horn Sound

The London Horn Sound is a 2006 album recorded by 32 horn players from the London area. The tracks span a variety of genres such as classical, pop, film soundtracks, and jazz. Here are some of my favorites.

  • Titanic medley
  • La Danza (Rossini)
  • Evening Prayer from Hansel and Gretel
  • Tico-Tico
  • Arrival of the Queen of Sheba (Handel)
  • Bohemian Rhapsody (Queen)
  • Caravan (Ellington)
